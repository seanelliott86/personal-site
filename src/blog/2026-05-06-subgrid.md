Subgrid is a game changer and makes your life a hell of a lot easier.\
\
If you inspect my site now your going to see a flatter structure and thats thanks to subgrid.

Previously I defined a reusable grid with multiple named columns. The main idea by using grid was so that I could have defined sections like full-bleed, pop-out and one for my content with a good line-length to help with readability. All my layouts in one place. Very quick, very easy and the idea was reinforced with articles like [Layout Breakouts with CSS Grid - Ryan Mulligan](https://ryanmulligan.dev/blog/layout-breakouts/). Grid is certainly helpful in that way. \
But this immediately revealed a problem. I hadnt used grid before for bigger layouts, I used it here or there when flex box just couldn't cope. Now that I had created a sitewide layout engine, each timeI wanted a new content section I had to reimplement that grid and then apply the desired width I wanted.\
Sure I made my layouts reusable with classes like \`.wrapper\` for my grid container and \`.popout\` or \`.fullone\` – best class name I've ever written btw – for aligning to certain columns. But that still meant on my homepage \`.wrapper\` was repeated four times. Not earth shattering but it just felt wasteful. 

"Why cant my grid work across everything, not just the direct children of the gird element" I recall thinking. \
This was in 2022 when in "designed" my site – FYI I'm a frontend developer and a poor designer – and subgrid was only available in Firefox. Life continued on from their with no issues. My site function and no one died because of my repeated use of \`.wrapper\`.

But now, I had an itch to do more to my site, I had seen subgrid being mentioned in a few newsletters and heard it was baseline, which means I decided to take a look to understand what problems does subgrid solve.

Well now I can define my grid at the top level and subgrid it to align to the columns i want
