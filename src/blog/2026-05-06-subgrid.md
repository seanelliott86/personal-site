Subgrid is a game changer and makes your life a hell of a lot easier.\
\
If you inspect my site now your going to see a flatter structure and thats thanks to subgrid.

Previously I defined a reusable grid with multiple named columns. The main idea by using grid was so that I could have defined sections like full-bleed, pop-out and one for my content with a good line-length to help with readability. Very quick, very easy and the idea was reinforced with articles like [Layout Breakouts with CSS Grid - Ryan Mulligan](https://ryanmulligan.dev/blog/layout-breakouts/). Grid is certainly helpful in that way. \
But this immediately showed a problem, for each section I had to reimplement that grid and then apply the desired width I wanted.\
Sure I made it reusable with classes like \`.wrapper\` for my grid container and \`.popout\` or \`.fullone\` (best class name ive ever written) for the columns I wanted that section to fit to. But that meant for my homepage \`.wrapper\` was repeated four times. Not earth shattering but it just felt wasteful, why can my grid work across everything, not just direct children of the gird element. This was in 2022 when in "designed" my site and subgrid was only available in Firefox.

I can define my grid at the top level and subgrid it to align to the columns i want
